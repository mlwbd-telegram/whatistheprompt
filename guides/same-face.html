How to Keep the Same Face in AI Images

Guide · 5 min read

One of the biggest challenges in AI image generation is keeping the same face consistent across different images. Whether you're creating character sheets, storyboards, or social media content, face consistency matters.

Why Does AI Change Faces?

AI image generators like Midjourney, Flux, and Stable Diffusion don't "remember" faces the way humans do. Each generation starts from random noise, and the prompt is the only guide the AI has. Without explicit face descriptors, the AI fills in details differently each time.

The Key: Detailed Face Descriptors

To preserve face identity, your prompt must include specific, measurable facial attributes:

  • Face shape: oval, round, square, heart-shaped, diamond
  • Skin tone: warm olive, cool fair, deep brown, golden tan
  • Eye shape & color: almond-shaped hazel eyes, round blue eyes
  • Nose structure: straight bridge, button nose, wide nostrils
  • Lip shape: full bow-shaped lips, thin natural pink lips
  • Hair details: color, length, texture, parting style, highlights
  • Distinctive features: freckles, dimples, eyebrow shape, jawline definition

💡 Pro Tip

The more specific your face description, the more consistent the results. Use 15–20 face-related keywords in every prompt for best results.
