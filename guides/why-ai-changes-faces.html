Why AI Changes Faces

Guide · 5 min read

If you've generated two AI images with the same prompt and gotten two completely different faces, you're not doing anything wrong. This is actually how AI image generators work by design — and understanding why is the key to fixing it.

The Randomness Problem

Every AI image generation starts with a canvas of pure random noise — like TV static. The AI then gradually removes noise, guided by your text prompt. But since the starting noise is random, each generation begins from a different point.

Even with an identical prompt, different starting noise produces different results. This is especially true for faces, which have the most variation potential.

How AI "Sees" Your Prompt

AI models don't read prompts like humans. They convert text into numerical vectors (called embeddings) that guide the denoising process. When you write "a woman with brown hair," the AI interprets this broadly — there are millions of possible "woman with brown hair" faces in its training data.

Without specific constraints, the AI picks a random interpretation each time.

The Fix: Constraint Through Specificity

The solution is to narrow the AI's interpretation space by adding constraints:

  • Instead of "brown eyes" use "warm dark-brown almond-shaped eyes with a slight upward tilt at the outer corners"
  • Instead of "nice smile" use "a gentle closed-mouth smile with a small dimple on the right cheek"
  • Instead of "nice face" use "oval face shape with high defined cheekbones, a straight nose bridge, and balanced facial proportions"

💡 The Specificity Paradox

The more detailed your description, the more consistent the results. Short prompts create random faces. Long, precise prompts lock identity.
