Best Prompt for Same Face AI

6 min read · All Levels

Writing the perfect prompt for face consistency isn't about one magic phrase — it's about structure. Here are the best prompt templates and the anatomy of what makes them work.

The Prompt Structure That Works

Every effective face-lock prompt follows this order:

[Subject description with extreme facial detail] + [Hair description with color, texture, style] + [Clothing and accessories] + [Pose and expression] + [Environment and background] + [Lighting and camera details] + [Technical quality tags: "ultra-realistic photography, 8K, sharp focus"] + [MANDATORY FACE LOCK STATEMENT]

The Mandatory Face Lock Statement

This is the most critical part. It should appear at the end of every prompt:

"Use the uploaded photo as the strict and binding face reference. You must preserve with absolute precision: the complete facial bone structure and geometry, the exact shape and size of each eye, the precise iris color, the exact nose shape, the exact lip shape, the precise jawline and chin shape, the exact cheekbone placement, the unique skin tone and undertone, and every unique identifying facial characteristic including any moles, freckles, or distinguishing features. The face must be an exact, unmistakable match to the reference photo."

Why This Works

  • Specificity: Every facial feature is described with precision, leaving no room for AI interpretation
  • Order matters: Face description comes first, so it anchors the generation
  • Technical tags: Terms like "ultra-realistic photography" and "sharp focus" guide the AI toward photorealism
  • Face lock: The explicit instruction at the end reinforces identity preservation

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't use vague descriptions like "pretty" or "attractive" — be specific
  • Don't skip the face lock statement — it's non-negotiable
  • Don't change the face description between generations — only change the background or clothing
  • Don't use stylized art terms if you want photorealism
